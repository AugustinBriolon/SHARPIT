# PRODUCT_VISION

## Vision

SHARPIT aims to become the world's reference Human Performance Intelligence System.

Its purpose is not to replace coaches, physicians or scientists.

Its purpose is to build the most accurate, explainable and continuously evolving understanding of an athlete's physiological state, enabling better decisions every day.

The long-term objective is to create an explainable Digital Twin capable of understanding the athlete, anticipating adaptation, simulating possible futures and supporting decisions with measurable confidence.

---

# The Problem

The modern athlete is surrounded by data but lacks understanding.

Wearables collect thousands of measurements every day.

Applications display dozens of metrics.

Dashboards become increasingly sophisticated.

Yet the athlete still struggles to answer fundamental questions:

- Am I adapting?
- Am I accumulating useful fatigue or harmful fatigue?
- Should I train today?
- Should I recover?
- Should I change my plan?
- Am I progressing toward my objective?
- What is limiting my performance?
- What is the next best decision?

SHARPIT exists to answer these questions.

---

# The Product

SHARPIT is not centered around workouts.

It is centered around the athlete.

Everything revolves around continuously estimating the athlete's current internal state.

Training sessions are observations.

Sleep is an observation.

Nutrition is an observation.

Stress is an observation.

Recovery is an observation.

Environmental conditions are observations.

Subjective feedback is an observation.

Together they refine the Digital Twin.

---

# The Digital Twin

The Digital Twin is the heart of SHARPIT.

It is a dynamic model representing the current state of an athlete.

It estimates variables that cannot be directly measured.

Examples include:

- accumulated fatigue
- recovery capacity
- adaptation potential
- readiness
- confidence
- resilience
- injury susceptibility
- training responsiveness

The Digital Twin is continuously updated as new information becomes available.

Every recommendation originates from this model.

---

# Core Capabilities

## Understand

Transform fragmented observations into coherent physiological understanding.

---

## Predict

Estimate how the athlete is likely to evolve over the coming days, weeks and months.

---

## Simulate

Answer "What happens if..."

Examples:

- increase weekly volume by 15%
- replace VO₂max with threshold work
- reduce sleep by one hour
- taper earlier
- increase strength frequency

The objective is to compare possible futures before acting.

---

## Recommend

Produce personalized recommendations.

Every recommendation must include:

- reasoning
- confidence
- expected benefit
- possible downside
- scientific rationale

---

## Learn

Continuously improve the Digital Twin using historical outcomes.

Learning must remain transparent and explainable.

---

# Product Principles

Every feature must improve the Digital Twin.

Every metric must improve a decision.

Every recommendation must be explainable.

Every prediction must expose uncertainty.

Every algorithm must be replaceable.

Every scientific assumption must be documented.

Long-term adaptation is always preferred over short-term optimization.

---

# Product Pillars

## Scientific Rigor

Models are grounded in evidence.

Scientific uncertainty is explicitly represented.

Evidence is continuously re-evaluated.

---

## Explainability

Nothing is hidden.

The athlete can understand every recommendation.

Confidence and limitations are always visible.

---

## Personalization

Every athlete is different.

Recommendations adapt to:

- physiology
- objectives
- history
- environment
- consistency
- preferences
- available equipment
- lifestyle

---

## Continuous Learning

The system evolves with the athlete.

Recommendations become more accurate over time.

The Digital Twin becomes progressively richer.

---

## Human-Centered Design

Technology exists to simplify decisions.

Never to increase cognitive load.

Interfaces should communicate understanding rather than data.
