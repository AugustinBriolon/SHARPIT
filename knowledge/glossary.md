# SHARPIT Glossary

> Every term used in SHARPIT — in the UI, in the code, in the AI coach, in recommendations — must be defined here.
> When a term appears in a recommendation, its meaning must match this glossary exactly.
> If the code and this glossary disagree, the code must be fixed.

---

## A

### Acute Training Load (ATL)

The exponentially weighted moving average of daily Training Stress Score over a 7-day time constant (τ=7). Represents recent training stress — what the body is currently fatigued from. Also called "fatigue" in the PMC model. Computed by `computePmcSeries()` in `analytics.ts`.

### Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio (ACWR)

The ratio of 7-day total training load to the average weekly load over the preceding 42 days. A measure of how much the current training week differs from the athlete's established baseline. Computed by `computeTrainingLoad()` in `training-load.ts`.

- ACWR <0.8: under-training
- ACWR 0.8–1.3: sweet spot (optimal progressive stimulus)
- ACWR 1.3–1.5: elevated risk zone
- ACWR >1.5: high injury risk zone

### Adaptation

The physiological process by which the body responds to training stress by becoming stronger, faster, or more efficient. The fundamental goal of all training. Requires both adequate stress (overload) and adequate recovery. SHARPIT tracks adaptation indirectly through CTL trends and performance record progression.

### Alert

A programmatic signal generated by `computeAlerts()` when one or more physiological indicators cross a defined threshold. Alerts have three severity levels: `info`, `warning`, `danger`. Alerts are deterministic — given the same data, the same alert always fires. See `alerts.ts`.

---

## B

### Block (Training Block)

A period of training with a consistent physiological focus — typically 3–5 weeks. Corresponds to one `PlanPhase` in SHARPIT's periodization model. Not to be confused with a mesocycle (which may contain multiple blocks).

### Body Battery

A Garmin-proprietary metric (0–100) representing estimated energy reserves. Computed by Garmin's algorithm from HRV, stress, activity, and sleep. SHARPIT treats this as an opaque sensor output, not a validated scientific measure. High body battery (≥70) correlates with good recovery; low (<40) with fatigue.

### Brick Session

A training session combining two consecutive sports without rest between them — typically cycling followed by running. Stored in SHARPIT as multiple linked `PlannedSession` records sharing a `brickGroupId`. The transition between sports is physiologically significant (metabolic and neuromuscular crossover).

---

## C

### Chronic Training Load (CTL)

The exponentially weighted moving average of daily Training Stress Score over a 42-day time constant (τ=42). Represents long-term fitness — the body's established aerobic capacity and load-bearing capacity. Also called "fitness" in the PMC model. CTL is slow-changing and represents the athlete's baseline capability. Computed by `computePmcSeries()` in `analytics.ts`.

### Confidence

In SHARPIT, the degree of certainty attached to a recommendation or prediction, reflecting data quality, model validity, and input completeness. See `confidence-scoring.md` for the full framework. A recommendation with low confidence must communicate its uncertainty explicitly.

### Critical Power (CP)

The maximal power output sustainable theoretically indefinitely under aerobic metabolism. Distinct from FTP (which is an empirical 60-minute proxy). SHARPIT does not currently implement a CP model but uses FTP as its primary cycling threshold.

### CTL — see Chronic Training Load

---

## D

### Daily Briefing

An AI-generated summary of the athlete's current state and recommended actions for the day. Synthesizes recovery signals, planned sessions, and recent training load into a cohesive narrative. Generated by `daily-briefing.ts`. Stored in the `DailyBriefing` database model.

### Data Quality

A classification of how trustworthy a data point is. SHARPIT distinguishes: `measured` (directly from sensor), `estimated` (computed from a model), `missing` (not available). See `data-quality.md`.

### Decoupling (Cardiac Decoupling)

The ratio of aerobic efficiency in the first half vs second half of a training session. Computed as the percentage difference in Efficiency Factor (watts/HR for cycling, speed/HR for running) between the two halves. Decoupling >5% indicates aerobic fitness limitation or excess intensity. Computed by `computeDecoupling()` in `activity-analysis.ts`.

### Deload

A planned reduction in training load to facilitate supercompensation and prevent accumulative fatigue. In SHARPIT's periodization model, deload weeks fire every 4 weeks during BASE and BUILD phases at 72% of normal load. Not evidence-based for exact values — see `training-load.md`.

---

## E

### Efficiency Factor (EF)

The ratio of power output (or pace) to heart rate. For cycling: watts / BPM. For running: speed (m/s) / BPM. Used to assess aerobic fitness and track cardiac drift within and across sessions. Higher EF indicates better aerobic economy.

---

## F

### Fatigue

In the PMC model: ATL (Acute Training Load) — the exponentially weighted recent training stress. Distinct from subjective fatigue (how the athlete feels). Also used colloquially to describe accumulated tiredness; SHARPIT distinguishes these by context.

### Feeling

The athlete's subjective post-session assessment, stored as a string in `Activity.feeling`. Free-text or structured categories (Garmin-derived values). Treated as primary data — equal in validity to sensor data for decision-making.

### Fitness

In the PMC model: CTL (Chronic Training Load). Represents the athlete's long-term aerobic adaptation and load-bearing capacity. Distinct from VO2max, FTP, or any single performance measure.

### Form

Training Stress Balance (TSB) = CTL - ATL. Represents the balance between accumulated fitness and current fatigue. High form means fit and fresh. TSB >+15: fresh/tapered. TSB -10 to +5: optimal training zone. See `recovery.ts:buildFormView()`.

### FTP (Functional Threshold Power)

The highest average power output an athlete can sustain for 60 minutes. SHARPIT estimates FTP using a factor model: best 20-minute power × 0.95, best 30-minute power × 0.95, best 60-minute power × 0.97. Standard Coggan methodology. Stored in `AthleteProfile.ftpW`.

---

## G

### Goal

An athlete-defined target. SHARPIT distinguishes two goal kinds:

- `RACE`: a specific competitive event with a date and optionally a target performance
- `METRIC`: a measurable performance target (distance, pace, power)

Goals also have priority: `A` (season priority), `B` (intermediate), `C` (test/training race).

---

## H

### Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

The variation in time between consecutive heartbeats. High HRV generally indicates good parasympathetic tone, recovery, and readiness. Low HRV or declining trends indicate accumulated stress, fatigue, illness, or overtraining. SHARPIT reads HRV from Garmin's overnight measurement (`DailyHealth.hrv`, `DailyHealth.hrvStatus`).

### HRV Status

Garmin's classification of the athlete's HRV relative to their personal baseline: `BALANCED` (normal, good), `UNBALANCED_LOW` (below baseline, flagged), `UNBALANCED_HIGH` (above baseline, can indicate deload recovery). The Garmin algorithm is proprietary; SHARPIT treats status as an opaque classification.

### Hybrid Athlete

An athlete who trains across endurance and strength disciplines concurrently. SHARPIT models strength training load using TSS approximations (0.7 TSS/minute) which are known to be physiologically incomplete. See `hybrid-athlete.md`.

---

## I

### Intensity Factor (IF)

The ratio of Normalized Power (or HR-based equivalent) to FTP (or LTHR). IF <0.75 = easy/endurance. IF 0.75–0.85 = tempo. IF 0.85–0.95 = threshold. IF >1.0 = above threshold/VO2max.

---

## L

### Load

The Training Stress Score (TSS) or TSS-equivalent for a given session or day. SHARPIT normalizes all sports to a common TSS unit for PMC and ACWR computation. For cycling: computed from power. For running/other: estimated from heart rate or duration × sport factor. Stored in `Activity.load` and `PlannedSession.load`. Units: TSS points.

### LTHR (Lactate Threshold Heart Rate)

The heart rate corresponding to the lactate threshold — the intensity above which lactate accumulates faster than it is cleared. Used as the reference point for HR zone calculation. Stored in `AthleteProfile.lthr`. If LTHR is unavailable, zone calculation degrades to estimated % of max HR.

---

## M

### Mesocycle

A training period of 3–6 weeks with a consistent goal (base fitness, building intensity, peaking). In SHARPIT's schema, corresponds to one or more consecutive `PlanWeek` records within a single `PlanPhase`.

### Microcycle

A single training week. The fundamental planning unit in SHARPIT's periodization system. Each `PlanWeek` represents one microcycle.

### Monotony

A measure of training variability. High monotony = low day-to-day variation in load = higher accumulated fatigue for the same total load. SHARPIT does not currently implement a monotony metric explicitly but ACWR trends reflect it indirectly.

---

## N

### Normalized Power (NP)

A weighted average of power output that better reflects the physiological cost of variable-intensity efforts than simple average power. Computed as: (mean of 30-second rolling averages raised to the 4th power)^(1/4). Implemented in `computeNormalizedPower()` in `activity-analysis.ts`. Higher NP vs average power indicates more variability (and higher metabolic cost).

---

## P

### Performance Management Chart (PMC)

The combination of CTL, ATL, and TSB plotted over time. The central training management visualization in SHARPIT. Implemented in `computePmcSeries()` in `analytics.ts`.

### Periodization

The structured organization of training across time into phases with different emphases, designed to produce peak performance at a target date. SHARPIT implements linear periodization: BASE → BUILD → PEAK → TAPER → RACE. Implemented in `periodization.ts`.

### Phase

A named period within a training plan with a specific physiological focus and load multiplier:

- `BASE` (0.85×): aerobic foundation, high volume, low intensity
- `BUILD` (1.0×): progressive overload, threshold work introduced
- `PEAK` (1.08×): maximum load, race-specific intensity
- `TAPER` (0.55×): load reduction while maintaining intensity, supercompensation
- `RACE` (0.25×): active recovery and competition

### Physical Note

SHARPIT's injury and physical condition tracking entity. Categories: `PAIN`, `INJURY`, `MOBILITY`, `POSTURE`. Has severity (0–10), status (ACTIVE/MONITORING/RESOLVED), side (LEFT/RIGHT/BILATERAL), and timestamped check-ins. Active injuries with severity ≥6 trigger `danger` alerts.

### Planned Session

A future training session with defined characteristics (type, date, intensity zone, duration, load). May be linked to a completed `Activity` via `session-linking.ts`. Part of a `TrainingPlan` via `PlanWeek`.

### Prediction

A SHARPIT output that estimates a future value (e.g., race time, FTP) from current data. Predictions carry confidence classifications. They are model outputs, not certainties.

### Proactive Action

An AI-suggested next step that SHARPIT generates from the current state of the athlete's data without being explicitly asked. Distinct from a recommendation in that it takes initiative. Generated by `computeProactiveActions()` in `proactive-coach.ts`.

## R

### Race

In SHARPIT terminology: a scheduled competitive event that drives periodization. Stored as a `Goal` with `kind=RACE`. Functions as both a performance target and as the anchor date for training plan generation.

### Readiness

The athlete's estimated capacity to absorb and benefit from training on a given day. In SHARPIT, derived primarily from Garmin Training Readiness Score (0–100). Thresholds: ≥75 = good, ≥50 = moderate, <50 = low. See `recovery.ts:buildReadinessView()`.

### Recovery

The physiological process by which the body repairs exercise-induced damage, restores glycogen, and consolidates adaptations. SHARPIT monitors recovery through: Garmin readiness score, HRV, resting heart rate, sleep quality, and TSB.

### Recovery Score — see Readiness

### Recommendation

A SHARPIT output suggesting a specific action (train hard, rest, reduce volume). Recommendations have a tone (good/moderate/low), a title, and an explanation. They are always traceable to the signals that produced them.

### Resting Heart Rate (RHR)

Heart rate measured at complete rest. Elevated RHR (≥5 bpm above personal baseline) is a signal of accumulated fatigue, stress, illness, or overtraining. SHARPIT computes baseline as the average of the preceding 7–14 days.

### Riegel Exponent

The exponent in the Riegel fatigue law used for race time prediction: T₂ = T₁ × (D₂/D₁)^1.06. Value 1.06 is used for recreational to trained runners. The exponent increases toward 1.09 for less-trained athletes and decreases toward 1.03 for elites. SHARPIT uses a fixed 1.06.

### RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion)

The athlete's subjective assessment of training intensity on a 1–10 scale. Stored in `Activity.rpe`. Treated as primary physiological data. Used as a fallback for load estimation when power/HR data is unavailable.

---

## S

### Session

Used interchangeably with `Activity` when referring to a completed training session. A `PlannedSession` is a future/intended session; an `Activity` is a completed one.

### Sleep Score

Garmin's composite score (0–100) for a night's sleep quality. The Garmin algorithm weights sleep stages, HRV, restlessness, and timing. SHARPIT treats it as an opaque instrument output. Thresholds in SHARPIT: ≥80 = good, ≥60 = moderate, <60 = low. See `sleep.ts`.

### Specificity

The principle that physiological adaptation is specific to the stress applied. Running fitness does not fully transfer to cycling. SHARPIT's load calculations treat different sports as comparable in TSS but not in specificity — sport-specific performance metrics (FTP, run threshold pace) are tracked separately.

### Strain

The accumulated training stress over a period. High strain without adequate recovery leads to overtraining. SHARPIT monitors strain through ATL and ACWR. Distinct from Fatigue (ATL is one week of strain; strain can be measured across longer periods).

### Supercompensation

The temporary state of elevated fitness that follows a period of training stress + adequate recovery. The goal of tapering. Characterized by elevated CTL and positive TSB. SHARPIT targets this state for race day.

---

## T

### Training Plan

A SHARPIT `TrainingPlan` database record containing a sequence of `PlanWeek` records organized into phases, from `startDate` to `raceDate`. Generated by `generateMacroPlan()` in `periodization.ts`.

### Training Readiness — see Readiness

### Training Stress Balance (TSB)

CTL - ATL. The mathematical difference between fitness and fatigue. SHARPIT interprets:

- TSB >+15: fresh/tapered, optimal for racing or testing
- TSB -10 to +5: optimal training zone (fit and slightly fatigued)
- TSB -10 to -30: accumulated fatigue (high training block)
- TSB <-30: significant overload (risk of overtraining)
  Implemented in `buildFormView()` in `recovery.ts`.

### Training Stress Score (TSS)

A standardized measure of the physiological cost of a training session. Normalized to: 1 hour at threshold intensity = 100 TSS. For cycling, computed from power (NP, IF, FTP). For running/other, estimated from HR or duration × sport factor. All SHARPIT load metrics normalize to TSS equivalents.

### Threshold Pace (Run)

The running pace corresponding to the lactate threshold — the pace the athlete can sustain for approximately 60 minutes at maximal effort. Stored in `AthleteProfile.runThresholdPaceSecPerKm`. Used for HR zone and TSS computation in running.

---

## V

### Variability Index (VI)

Normalized Power / Average Power for a cycling session. VI close to 1.00 indicates a steady-state effort (time trial). VI >1.05 indicates a variable effort (hills, racing). Used to assess pacing strategy and metabolic cost. Computed in `activity-analysis.ts`.

### VO2max

Maximal oxygen uptake — the maximum rate at which the body can consume oxygen during exercise. The highest-level predictor of endurance potential. Stored in `AthleteProfile.vo2maxRunning` and `AthleteProfile.vo2maxCycling`. Currently imported from Garmin; not independently computed by SHARPIT.

### Verdict

SHARPIT's consolidated daily training recommendation synthesizing all available signals. Generated by `buildTrainingVerdict()` in `dashboard.ts`. The single most important output the athlete sees each day.

---

## W

### Weekly Review

An AI-generated weekly summary analyzing the training week, comparing planned vs completed sessions, noting trends, and recommending focus for the following week. Generated by `weekly-review.ts`. Stored in the `WeeklyReview` database model.

### Workout

See Session / Activity. In SHARPIT, the preferred term for a structured training session is `Session`.
